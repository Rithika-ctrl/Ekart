1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that a website places on your device (computer, phone, or tablet) when you visit. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to site owners.

Ekart uses both first-party cookies (set by Ekart directly) and a small number of third-party cookies (set by services we use, such as payment processors or social login providers). Cookies are not viruses or malware — they are simple data files that help us deliver a better experience.

Ekart does not use cookies for third-party advertising or to track your activity on other websites.

2. How Ekart Uses Cookies

Ekart uses cookies and server-side sessions for the following purposes:

  • To keep you logged in to your customer, vendor, or delivery partner account as you navigate the site
  • To maintain your shopping cart contents across page loads and browser tab switches
  • To remember your recently viewed products for a better browsing experience
  • To handle OAuth2 social login sessions (Google, Facebook, GitHub) securely via Spring Security
  • To maintain session state for the AI chatbot conversation context
  • To protect the platform against cross-site request forgery (CSRF) attacks
  • To store your active session identifier so you do not need to re-login on every page

3. Types of Cookies We Use

Essential

Required

Necessary for the platform to function. Includes login session, cart, and CSRF protection. Cannot be disabled without breaking core functionality.

Authentication

Required

Used by Spring Security and OAuth2 to identify your active login session and keep you authenticated across the platform.

Preference

Optional

Remember your recently viewed products and UI preferences to give you a personalised browsing experience on subsequent visits.

Analytics

Optional

Help us understand how users interact with the platform (pages visited, actions taken). Used internally to improve the product. No third-party analytics provider currently used.

5. User Consent

When you first visit Ekart, you will see a cookie consent notice at the bottom of the screen:

We use cookies to keep you logged in and improve your experience. Essential cookies are required. Learn more

By clicking Accept All, you consent to both essential and optional (preference, analytics) cookies. By clicking Essential Only, only cookies required for the platform to function will be set.

Continuing to browse without interacting with the banner is considered implicit acceptance of essential cookies only, which are strictly necessary for the service to operate.

You may withdraw or change your cookie preferences at any time via your browser settings or by contacting us.

6. Managing Your Cookies

You have full control over cookies through your browser settings. Here is how to manage them in common browsers:

  • Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data
  • Mozilla Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data
  • Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data
  • Safari: Preferences → Privacy → Manage Website Data
  • Mobile (Android/iOS): Browser app Settings → Privacy → Cookies

Important: Blocking or deleting essential cookies (especially JSESSIONID) will prevent you from staying logged in and will clear your shopping cart. Core functionality requires these cookies.

7. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on Ekart are set by third-party services we integrate with. We do not control these cookies and they are governed by the respective third party's privacy policy:

  • Razorpay — May set cookies during the payment checkout process for fraud prevention and session continuity. Razorpay Privacy Policy →
  • Google OAuth2 — Sets cookies when you use "Sign in with Google" for authentication. Google Privacy Policy →
  • Facebook OAuth2 — Sets cookies when you use "Sign in with Facebook". Facebook Privacy Policy →
  • GitHub OAuth2 — Sets cookies when you use "Sign in with GitHub". GitHub Privacy Policy →

Ekart does not currently use Google Analytics, Facebook Pixel, or any third-party advertising or tracking cookies.
